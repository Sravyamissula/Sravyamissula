About Singapore

Singapore, officially known as the Republic of Singapore, is a city-state and island nation in Southeast Asia. It is located at the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula, separated from Malaysia by the Johor Strait and from Indonesia by the Singapore Strait. Despite its small size of about 728.6 square kilometers (as of 2024), Singapore is renowned for its economic strength, efficient governance, and multicultural society.

Famous Places In Singapore

Historical Places in Singapore

Fort Canning Park

Fort Canning has been a landmark for centuries, serving as the seat of ancient Malay rulers, a British military base, and the site where Singapore's surrender to the Japanese was decided during World War II.

Raffles Hotel

Established in 1887, Raffles Hotel is an iconic colonial-era luxury hotel where famous figures like Charlie Chaplin and Rudyard Kipling once stayed.

Chinatown

Established in the early 19th century as a settlement for Chinese immigrants, Chinatown reflects the cultural and architectural heritage of Singapore’s Chinese community.

National Museum of Singapore

The oldest museum in Singapore, established in 1887, showcasing the country’s history, culture, and evolution through engaging exhibits.

Changi Chapel and Museum

Dedicated to Singapore’s World War II history, it commemorates the experiences of prisoners of war (POWs) held at Changi Prison during the Japanese occupation.
