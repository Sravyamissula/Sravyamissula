About India

India is a vast country in South Asia, known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse traditions, and historical significance. It is home to many ancient civilizations and is the birthplace of several major religions, including Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. India is also famous for its monuments, beautiful landscapes, and colorful festivals that attract millions of visitors each year.

Famous Places In India

Historical Places in India

Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh

An architectural gem and one of the most exquisite historical places in India is Fatehpur Sikri, a town in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh. A recognized UNESCO World Heritage Site, Fatehpur Sikri is made predominantly out of red sandstone, and dates back to the late 16th century, when it was founded by Mughal Emperor Akba

Victoria Memorial

Ranking amongst the top 10 historical places in India, Kolkata’s Victoria Memorial was constructed in honour of Queen Victoria, making it one of the relics of the British Raj. Built entirely out of white marble, the Victoria Memorial is almost a replica of its namesake in London

Humayun’s Tomb, Delhi

Amongst the oldest historical places in India, Humayun’s Tomb in Delhi, along the banks of the Yamuna River, was also the first garden-tomb in the country. Built by Emperor Humayun’s wife in memory of her husband in the 16th century, this monument is surrounded by double-storied gateways and four gardens, and looks simply phenomenal.

Khajuraho Temples, Madhya Pradesh

A symbol of India's rich history, located in Delhi, it was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for around 200 years.

Golden Temple

The holiest shrine for Sikhs, located in Amritsar, known for its stunning gold-covered structure and spiritual significance.

Customs and Traditions of India

Diwali - Festival of Lights

Celebrated across India, Diwali symbolizes the triumph of light over darkness, with vibrant lamps, fireworks, and joyous festivities.

Holi - Festival of Colors

Known as the festival of colors, Holi brings people together to celebrate love, unity, and the arrival of spring with colorful powders and water.

Bharatanatyam - Classical Dance

A classical Indian dance form that combines music, expression, and rhythm to depict stories from mythology and ancient scriptures.
